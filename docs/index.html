This course is an introduction to the linguistics of East and Southeast Asian languages. The course has the following objectives:

  • The course introduces the largest language groups and the context in which they are spoken and introduces students to basic literature.
  • Part of the course will be work with texts from several Asian languages, as well as the collection and analysis of own data.

After completing the course, students will be able to distinguish and compare language groups in this area. Furthermore, they will be able to understand the current relationships between language groups, and between official and regional or tribal languages. Students will systematically study one grammatical or linguistic phenomenon in the selected language or languages â€‹â€‹in this area and will write a seminar paper about it, in which they will demonstrate both knowledge of specialist literature and the ability to analyze and describe the selected phenomenon.

Seminar work can be developed individually or in groups so that it is clear which part each student is responsible for. In the last week, students prepare a short informative presentation about their work for their colleagues.

Assessment

The prerequisites for successful completion are the following:

  • Regular attendance and active participation in class
  • Presentation on a minority language
  • Random notes (6 observations of things related to the course topic that surprised you in the discussion forum)
  • Presentation on the chosen topic (final week)
  • Seminar paper on the chosen topic (range 5-8 pages)
